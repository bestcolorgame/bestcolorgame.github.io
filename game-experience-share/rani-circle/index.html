The Beauty of Rani Circle

Rani Circle is a picturesque roundabout located in the heart of Mysore, Karnataka, India. This iconic landmark is not only a traffic hub but also a symbol of the rich cultural heritage and architectural beauty of Mysore.

History

Rani Circle, also known as Jayachamarajendra Wadiyar Circle, was named after the last ruler of Mysore, Jayachamarajendra Wadiyar. It was built in the early 20th century during the reign of the Wadiyar dynasty. The circle features a central garden with a statue of a queen, surrounded by lush greenery and beautiful flowers.

Architecture

The architecture of Rani Circle reflects the Indo-Saracenic style, which combines elements of Indian and Islamic architecture. The intricate designs, arches, and domes add to the grandeur of the circle, making it a popular spot for both locals and tourists.

Attractions

Aside from its architectural beauty, Rani Circle is also known for the vibrant cultural events and celebrations that take place here. The circle is often decorated with lights and garlands during festivals like Dasara, adding to its charm and beauty.

Visiting Rani Circle

If you are planning a trip to Mysore, make sure to visit Rani Circle and soak in the beauty of this iconic landmark. You can take a leisurely stroll around the circle, admire the architecture, and enjoy the peaceful surroundings. Don't forget to capture some photos to preserve the memories of your visit.
