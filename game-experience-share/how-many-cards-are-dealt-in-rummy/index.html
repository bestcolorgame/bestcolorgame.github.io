How Many Cards are Dealt in Rummy

Rummy is a popular card game that is enjoyed by people of all ages around the world. One of the key aspects of playing rummy is the dealing of cards at the beginning of the game. The number of cards dealt to each player can vary depending on the type of rummy being played. Let's take a look at how many cards are typically dealt in some common variations of rummy:

Indian Rummy:

In Indian Rummy, 13 cards are dealt to each player. The game is usually played with 2 decks of standard playing cards, including the jokers. The goal of the game is to form valid sets and sequences with the cards you are dealt.

Gin Rummy:

In Gin Rummy, 10 cards are dealt to each player. The game is typically played with 1 deck of standard playing cards without the jokers. The objective of Gin Rummy is to form sets and runs, and then to "knock" or declare your hand.

Rummy 500:

In Rummy 500, 7 cards are dealt to each player. This variation is also played with 1 deck of standard playing cards without the jokers. The aim of the game is to reach 500 points by forming sets and runs.

Regardless of the variation of rummy being played, the basic concept remains the same - to arrange the cards in your hand into valid sets and sequences. The number of cards dealt sets the foundation for the game and players' strategies.
