How to Choose Hair Colors That Suit You

Choosing the right hair color isn't just about picking a shade you like; it's about finding the best match for your skin tone, eye color, and personal style. Here’s a guide to help you discover hair colors that will look amazing on you!

Analyze Your Skin Tone

Understanding different skin tones

To choose the perfect hair color, first understand your skin’s undertone. Skin undertones are categorized into three types: cool, warm, and neutral. A simple way to determine your skin’s undertone is by looking at the veins in your wrist. If they appear blue or purple, you have cool undertones. If they look green, you have warm undertones. If it’s hard to tell, you likely have a neutral undertone.

Matching Hair Colors with Skin Tones

Cool Undertones: Opt for hair colors with cool tones such as ash blond, platinum, black, and burgundy. These colors compliment cool undertones by balancing and not overpowering the natural skin hue.

Warm Undertones: Enhance your warm skin tone with shades like golden blonde, rich auburn, honey, and warm black. They add a natural-looking warmth and glow to your appearance.

Neutral Undertones: If you’re among the lucky few with neutral undertones, you can experiment with a wide range of colors from cool to warm tones. However, avoid colors that are too ashy or too golden.

Selecting colors based on skin undertones

Consider Your Eye Color

Your eye color can also guide you in choosing a hair color that highlights your eyes. For instance, rich, deep colors like dark brown or vibrant auburn can make blue eyes pop, while honey, blonde, or caramel tones can enhance the warmth in brown eyes.

Experiment With Semi-Permanent Dyes

If you’re unsure about committing to a permanent hair color change, consider trying a semi-permanent dye first. These dyes can give you a good idea of what the new color will look like and usually wash out after about 28 shampoos.

Consult a Professional

If you're still unsure about which hair color to choose, consulting a professional hair colorist can be a great step. They have the expertise to suggest what colors will best suit your skin and eye color, and how to achieve it while maintaining healthy hair.

Conclusion

Finding the right hair color is a balance of art and science. By understanding your skin's undertones and considering your eye color, you can make informed decisions. Remember, these tips are just guidelines and experimenting with your hair color can be a fun and rejuvenating experience!
