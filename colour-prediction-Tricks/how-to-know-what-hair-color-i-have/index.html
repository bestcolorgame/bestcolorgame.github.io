Understanding Your Hair Color

Identifying your own hair color might seem straightforward, but the nuances of tone, shade, and highlights can make it a bit more complex. Whether you're looking to find the perfect hair dye or just curious about defining your natural hue, here’s a simple guide to help you understand and describe your hair color accurately.

Step 1: Find Natural Light

Before you start evaluating your hair color, find a source of natural lighting as artificial lights can alter how colors appear. Stand near a window or go outside on a sunny day for the best lighting condition.

Step 2: Look at the Roots

For the most accurate color, look at the roots of your hair. The roots usually show your natural hair color, especially if the rest of your hair has been exposed to sunlight or chemical treatments which can slightly change the hair color. Use a handheld mirror along with a bathroom mirror to get a clear view of your hair’s roots.

Step 3: Determine the Base Color

Observe the overall color of your hair, which is your base color. Hair colors are generally classified under black, brown, blonde, red, and grey. Your hair might fall anywhere within these shades, from jet black to light blonde. Here are some common terms used in hair color:

  • Black: Can range from soft black to deep blue-black.
  • Brown: Includes light brown, amber, or even dark chocolate tones.
  • Blonde: Can vary from golden blonde to a pale platinum.
  • Red: Includes a spectrum from deep copper to light ginger.
  • Grey: Silver to salt-and-pepper shades.

Step 4: Identify Undertones

Hair color isn’t just about the visible shade; undertones can greatly influence how colors look against your skin. Cool undertones might appear ash or have hints of blue or green, while warm undertones could have hints of gold, red, or orange. Neutral undertones are a balance between these. Sunlight can help reveal these subtleties better.

Step 5: Consider Getting a Professional Opinion

If you're still unsure about your hair color or wish to make changes, consider visiting a professional hairstylist. They can offer the most accurate assessment and provide advice on which colors might suit you according to your natural shade.

Conclusion

Recognizing your exact hair color can be a fun discovery and is also practical for a variety of uses, from fashion decisions to hair care. Remember, your hair color can change over time, so you might find it switches shades as you grow older. Regular checks in natural lighting are the best way to keep track of these changes!

Tip:

Consider taking a picture of your hair in natural light and comparing it to a hair color chart online to get a closer idea of your shade.
