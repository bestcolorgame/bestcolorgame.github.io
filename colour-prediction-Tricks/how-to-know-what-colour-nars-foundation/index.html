How to Choose the Right NARS Foundation Shade

Finding the perfect foundation shade can be a daunting task, especially with brands like NARS that offer a wide range of colors and undertones. To ensure a seamless match, you need to consider several factors such as skin tone, undertone, and the type of finish you prefer. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to identify the best NARS foundation color for your skin.

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

Your skin tone is the surface skin color, which can range from fair to deep. NARS foundations cater to a broad spectrum, ensuring there's a shade for everyone.

Tip: Look at your jawline in natural light to determine your true skin color.

Step 2: Understand Your Skin’s Undertone

Undertones are the colors underneath the surface of your skin and can be classified as cool, warm, or neutral:

  • Cool: Pink, red, or bluish undertones.
  • Warm: Yellow, peachy, golden undertones.
  • Neutral: A mix of both cool and warm undertones.

Tip: Silver jewelry typically complements cool undertones, while gold looks great on warm undertones. If both suit you, you might have neutral undertones.

Step 3: Select the Right Foundation Formula

NARS offers various formulas ranging from sheer to full coverage and matte to luminous finishes. Your choice should depend on your skin type and the finish you prefer.

  • Sheer Glow Foundation: Ideal for a natural, radiant finish.
  • All Day Luminous Weightless Foundation: Perfect for long-wearing, full coverage without the weight.
  • Soft Matte Complete Foundation: Great for controlling oil and reducing the appearance of pores.

Step 4: Test the Foundation

When possible, test the foundation on your skin. Apply a swipe from the center of your face, blending outward. The right shade should disappear into your skin without looking ashy or overly bright.

Step 5: Check the Foundation Under Different Lighting

Lighting can dramatically affect how your foundation looks. Check your makeup under natural light, and if possible, with indoor lights to ensure it looks natural under different conditions.

Step 6: Consider Seasonal Changes

Your skin tone can change with the seasons. You might need a lighter shade for the winter months and a darker shade during the summer. It’s important to reassess your foundation shade as the year progresses or if your environment changes.

Conclusion

Choosing the right NARS foundation shade involves understanding your skin’s tone and undertone, selecting the appropriate formula, and testing the foundation under various lights. By following these steps, you can achieve a flawless foundation match that looks natural and highlights your best features.
