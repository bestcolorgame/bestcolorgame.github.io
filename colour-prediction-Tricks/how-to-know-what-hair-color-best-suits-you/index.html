Choosing the Perfect Hair Color That Suits You

Below, discover some key considerations and tips to help you find the perfect hair color that suits you best.

Analyze Your Skin Tone

Identifying your skin undertone is a crucial step in selecting your ideal hair color. Skin undertones are categorized as cool, warm, or neutral:

  • Cool: Pink, red, or bluish undertones.
  • Warm: Yellow, peachy, golden undertones.
  • Neutral: A mix of both cool and warm undertones or very balanced.

If you have cool undertones, shades like ash blonde, cool brunette, or jet black can enhance your complexion. For warm undertones, consider gold, caramel, honey for blondes, or rich auburn, and warm brunette shades. Those with neutral undertones have the flexibility to try colors from either spectrum, but should typically aim for more muted shades that don't overpower their balanced undertone.

Consider Your Eye Color

Your eye color can also determine what hair color will look best on you. Certain hair colors can make your eyes pop and look more striking. For example:

  • Brown eyes: Rich chocolates and deep burgundies.
  • Blue eyes: Blonde hues especially ash or platinum to create contrast.
  • Green eyes: Hues of red and auburn or dusty shades of brown.
  • Hazel eyes: Versatile color spectrum, from rich mochas to lighter caramel.

Age and Style

Your age and personal style should also play a role in your choice. As we age, our skin tone might change and some colors might not flatter as they once did. Lighter tones often help to soften features, which may be desirable for those wishing to downplay signs of aging.

Moreover, consider your wardrobe and the colors you feel best in. If you prefer bold and loud clothing, a vibrant colored hair might be right up your alley. However, if you're someone with a more subdued style, natural hair colors might be more suitable.

Maintenance and Commitment

Think about how much time and maintenance you are willing to invest. Lighter colors typically require more frequent touch-ups to manage roots, especially if your natural hair color is quite dark. Vivid colors such as red or blue might fade quickly and can require specialized products to maintain their brilliance.

Choosing the right hair color can be a fun and empowering process. Consider these factors and take the chance to express yourself through your hair color. When in doubt, consult with a professional colorist who can offer personalized advice based on your features and lifestyle needs.
