Determining Your Hair Color

Understanding your natural hair color can help you make better decisions about dying or treating your hair. Here's how you can figure out the true color of your hair.

1. Check Your Hair in Natural Light

To truly understand your hair color, observe it in natural daylight. Sunlight provides the clearest light to define the true color of your hair. Artificial lighting can distort the appearance of your hair color, being either too warm or too cool, thus misleading your perception.

2. Use a Color Chart

Compare your hair against a hair color chart, which you can find at salons or online. These charts often range from black, dark brown, light brown, blonde, to red, noting various shades in between. Hold a section of your hair close to the chart to determine the closest match.

3. Analyze the Roots

Examine the color of the new growth at the roots. This area is less likely to have been affected by sun exposure, washing, or hair treatments and usually shows your true natural hair color.

5. Ask a Professional

If you're still unsure about your hair color, consider consulting with a hair professional. They can offer a seasoned eye and even highlight subtleties that you might not notice yourself.

6. Consider Other Features

Your eye color and skin tone can also provide hints about your natural hair color. Often, these features align to form a harmonious look, indicating whether your hair is warm, cool, or neutral toned.

Understanding your hair color is not just about matching categories but embracing the unique shades that make you "you." Whether enhancing your natural beauty or planning a new hair color, knowing your base shade is the right step forward!
