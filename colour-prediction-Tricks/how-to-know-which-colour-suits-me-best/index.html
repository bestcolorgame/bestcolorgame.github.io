How to Know Which Colour Suits You Best

Discovering the perfect colors for your wardrobe that complement your skin tone, hair color, and eye color.

Understanding Color Theory

Before choosing colors, it's helpful to understand the basics of color theory. Colors can be divided into warm, cool, and neutral tones. This theory helps to determine which colors will suit you the best.

Determine Your Skin Tone

Identifying whether your skin tone is cool, warm, or neutral is the first step. People with cool skin tones have pink, red, or bluish undertones. Warm skin tones feature yellow, peachy, golden undertones, whereas neutral skin tones have a mix of both.

Color Suggestions for Different Skin Tones

Cool Skin Tones

Opt for colors like blue, purple, emerald green, and cool shades of grey. You might consider wearing silver jewelry as it typically complements cooler skin tones better.

Warm Skin Tones

Choose earthy colors such as red, peach, coral, orange, amber, and gold. For jewelry, gold pieces often enhance warm skin tones beautifully.

Neutral Skin Tones

If you have a neutral skin tone, most colors will be flattering on you. However, tailored shades often look particularly striking, including jade green, lagoon blue, and dusty pink.

Consistent Testing

To reliably gauge how a color looks on you, hold clothing or fabric swatches close to your face in natural light. This practice helps in visualizing if a particular color makes your face brighten up or look overshadowed.
