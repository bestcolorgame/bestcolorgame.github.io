How to Choose the Right Color Lens that Suits You

Choosing the right color for your lenses can be a fun yet challenging decision. Not only can colored lenses enhance your natural eye color or completely transform your look, but they can also complement your skin tone and hair color, reflecting your personal style.

Consider Your Skin Tone

The first step in choosing the right lens color is to consider your skin tone. Skin tones are typically categorized into warm, cool, or neutral categories.

  1. Warm Skin Tones: If you have a golden, olive, or darker complexion with greenish or yellow undertones, you likely have a warm skin tone. For warm skin tones, lens colors like light brown, hazel, honey, or green can enhance your look.
  2. Cool Skin Tones: If your skin has blue or pink undertones and you burn easily in the sun, you likely have a cool skin tone. Cool-colored lenses like blue, grey, and purple are stunning choices for this skin type.
  3. Neutral Skin Tones: With a mix of both warm and cool undertones, almost every color of lens can complement your skin. However, especially vibrant colors like brilliant blue, sterling grey, or gemstone green can make a striking impact.

Evaluate Your Natural Eye Color

Besides skin tone, your natural eye color can also dictate which lens color might suit you best:

  • Light Eyes (Blue or Green): To deepen and intensify your natural color, choose lens colors that are close to your eye color but richer. Consider colors like deep blue, emerald green, or even a grey that adds a smoky depth.
  • Dark Eyes (Brown or Hazel): You can opt for lenses that lighten or alter your natural hue significantly. Vibrant greens, blues, and even amethyst or turquoise are great for a striking new look.

Account for Hair Color

Your hair color should also be taken into account when choosing lens colors:

  • Blonde Hair: Blue, green, or violet lenses can provide a beautiful contrast and add a touch of coolness to your overall look.
  • Brown Hair: You can enhance your features with warm-colored lenses such as light brown or hazel, or cool it down with blues and grays.
  • Black or Dark Brown Hair: Bold colors like amethyst, green, or bright blue can add a dramatic flair to your ensemble.
  • Red Hair: Enhance your fiery look with green or brown lenses, or add a bit of mystique with grey lenses.

Experiment and Consult

If you're unsure which color to choose, consulting with an eye care professional can help. They can provide samples for you to try on and see how different colors interact with your natural features.

Remember: The key to choosing the right contact lens color is experimenting with various hues and considering how they complement your skin tone, natural eye color, and hair color. Don't be afraid to try something new!
