How to Know if a Hair Color Will Look Good on You

Choosing a new hair color can be exciting but also daunting. The fear of not knowing whether a color will suit you can make the decision challenging. With some knowledge and guidance, however, you can make a more informed choice that enhances your natural beauty. Here’s how to determine if a new hair color will look good on you.

1. Consider Your Skin Tone

The first step in choosing the right hair color is to consider your skin tone. Skin tones can be broadly classified into three categories: cool, warm, and neutral. A simple way to determine your skin tone is to look at the veins in your wrist under natural light. If your veins appear blue or purple, you have cool undertones. If they appear green, you have warm undertones. If it’s hard to tell, you likely have neutral undertones.

  • Cool undertones: Opt for hair colors with cool undertones such as ash blond, platinum, mocha, burgundy, and jet black.
  • Warm undertones: Choose warm shades like golden blonde, copper, rich chocolate, or caramel.
  • Neutral undertones: You’re in luck! Most hair colors will complement your skin tone.

2. Look at Your Eye Color

Your eye color can also serve as a guide for choosing a hair color. Certain hair colors can make your eye color pop and look more striking.

  • Blue eyes: Golden blonde, ash blonde, and colors with cool undertones can highlight blue eyes beautifully.
  • Brown eyes: Rich browns, burgundy, and chestnut are great choices.
  • Green eyes: Shades of red, auburn, and rich browns can enhance green eyes.

3. Consider Your Natural Hair Color

Starting with a color close to your natural hair color can be a safer choice, especially if you’re coloring your hair for the first time. Extreme changes can require heavy maintenance and might not always look natural. If you have dark hair, lighter shades may require bleaching which can damage your hair.

4. Test With a Wig or Virtual Tools

Before committing to a new hair color, try using a wig or virtual makeover tools. Many hair care brands and apps offer virtual tools where you can upload your photo and see how different hair colors will look on you. This can be a fun and risk-free way to experiment with different shades.

5. Seek Professional Advice

Consulting with a professional colorist can provide personalized advice tailored to your features, skin tone, and lifestyle. A colorist can also recommend high-quality products and treatments suited for colored hair, ensuring that your hair remains healthy and vibrant.

Conclusion

While choosing a new hair color is a personal and sometimes subjective decision, considering factors like your skin tone, eye color, and natural hair color can help guide you to colors that will likely look good on you. Remember, the most important thing is to choose a color that makes you feel confident and happy!
